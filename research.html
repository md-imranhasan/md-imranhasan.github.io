Key Research Projects

Antibiotic Resistance in Staphylococcus aureus

Investigated antibiotic resistance mechanisms in Staphylococcus aureus using computational biology.

  • Analyzed 1062 whole-genome sequences to identify antimicrobial resistance (AMR) genes.
  • Built gene interaction networks to identify critical genes (mgrA, arlR, sarA) which driving antimicrobial resistance offers opportunities to target the development of effective strategies against antimicrobial resistant bacteria
  • Identifying specific AMR(Antimicrobial resistance) genes withresistance mechanisms (e.g., efflux pumps, target modification, and target protection) and drug classes (e.g.,penams, tetracyclines) provides an improved understanding of key parts of multidrug resistance.

HIV Biomarker & Drug Target Identification

Utilized single-cell RNA sequencing (scRNA-seq) to analyze immune cell gene expression in HIV patients. Identified key differentially expressed genes (DEGs) through Seurat (R), and unraveling HIV Pathogenesis through identification of novel biomarkers, regulatory networks, and potential therapeutic targets.

  • Discovered ISG15, STAT1, and MX1 as potential biomarkers.
  • Performed Gene Ontology (GO) & KEGG pathway analysis to classify DEGs.
  • Developed molecular docking models to identify promising antiviral drug candidates.

Research Assistant (Summer Intern) - MicroRNA & Vaccine Response

Worked on a project exploring the role of microRNAs (miRNAs) in COVID-19 and respiratory diseases using bioinformatics and machine learning.

  • Applied linear regression models (R) to identify miRNAs associated with disease severity.
  • Integrated single-cell and bulk RNA sequencing data to study miRNA-mRNA interactions.
  • Developed machine learning models (Random Forest, SVM, Gradient Boosting) to predict vaccine response.
  • Identified miRNAs linked to immune modulation & inflammation, helping advance personalized medicine.

Research Experiences

Computational Drug Discovery & Disease Modeling

During my undergraduate studies, I participated in multiple research projects integrating computational and experimental methods to address biomedical challenges. Below are some key projects:

1. COVID-19 & Mucormycosis Co-Infection Research

Investigated the therapeutic potential of Galangin for COVID-19 and Mucormycosis co-infection. This research identified key host signaling pathways and molecular mechanisms targeted by Galangin, showcasing its role in reducing inflammation and tissue damage.

2. AI-Driven Colorectal Cancer Detection

Developed a deep convolutional neural network (DCNN)-based system for histopathological image analysis, achieving 99.80% accuracy in detecting colon adenocarcinoma. This highlights the potential of AI-powered diagnosis in pathology.

3. Systems Biology Approach to Multi-Disease Interactions

Explored the molecular links between Type 2 Diabetes, Tuberculosis, and Rheumatoid Arthritis using systems biology. Identified key regulatory networks and therapeutic targets to guide future drug development.

4. Blood-Based Transcriptomics for COVID-19

Conducted an in-depth transcriptomic analysis of blood samples from COVID-19 patients to identify differentially expressed genes and pivotal protein pathways. The findings contribute to better disease monitoring and targeted therapies.

5. AI-Assisted Diagnosis of Black Fungal Infections

Designed a transfer learning-based CNN model to detect black fungal infections. Optimized architectures like Xception, enhancing diagnostic accuracy for infectious diseases.
